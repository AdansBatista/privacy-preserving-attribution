THEOREM 2b — AMOUNT-DISCLOSURE BOUND  (k-independent)
------------------------------------------------------
  Tier 1 (alpha_a=0.05): Adv_amount <= 0.9993  [feasible if <= 0.10]
  Tier 2 (alpha_a=0.50): Adv_amount <= 1.0000
  Tier 3 (alpha_a=0.94): Adv_amount <= 1.0000

Interpretation (Theorem 2b):
  * Cohort size k does NOT appear in this bound — the DP guarantee
    is per-neighboring-database and is not diluted by enlarging
    the cohort.
  * Feasibility for amount disclosure requires
    alpha_a + tanh(eps_P/2) + delta_total < beta_amount.
  * At the headline (eps=1.0, P=12), tanh(eps_P/2) alone exceeds
    beta_amount=0.10. Meaningful amount-disclosure privacy at this
    budget therefore requires either a tighter per-period eps OR a
    relaxed beta_amount; this is a real constraint of standard
    Gaussian-DP and is part of the paper's negative result.

HEADLINE SUMMARY
================================================================

  Theorem 2a event-detection k* (Tier 1 alpha_e=0.05,
    sigma_T=$1000):                            3,551
  Theorem 2a Tier 2/3 (alpha_e >= beta_event): INFEASIBLE
  Theorem 2b amount-disclosure bound at Tier 1: 0.9993
  Theorem 2b amount-disclosure bound at Tier 3: 1.0000

Key results:
  1. The 1/sqrt(k) scaling of the event-detection bound emerges from
     cohort transaction variance, NOT DP composition. The original
     manuscript's 1/sqrt(rho*k) scaling under Yeom-style membership
     inference was a category error.

  2. The amount-disclosure bound is k-independent. Standard Gaussian
     DP at eps=1.0 / P=12 gives tanh(eps_P/2) ~= 0.95, dominating
     beta_amount=0.10. Meaningful per-record amount privacy under
     this budget is feasible only for very weak adversaries
     (alpha_a + tanh(eps_P/2) + delta < beta_amount).

  3. The two bounds quantify different threats. The mechanism
     satisfies the application-layer privacy goal iff BOTH bounds
     are met under the deployer's auxiliary-info assumptions.
